Part II - (ProsperLoan Data Exploration)¶

by (Melvin Ohabuike)¶

Investigation Overview¶

The goal of this data exploration of ProsperLoan was to make findings of employment playing a role in loan pay off targets which its proven to be true.

  • My choice of insights was basically to know if being employed plays a role on loan pay off. So i delved more into Employment

    status, Stated Monthly Amount, Loan Original Amount and Loan status.

  • There are conditions like borrowers Income needs to be verified before loan is being granted.

  • Borrowers do not need to be a home owner

  • Most borrowers' occupations (excluding the others group which could be freelancers, businessowners, affliate marketers etc) are: Professional, Computer Engineer and Executive.

    • Terms of Loans are mostly between 1-5years

    • How borrowers APR affects the lenders yeild and prosperscore and many more.

Dataset Overview¶

  • There are 113,937 entries of prosper loan in the dataset with 81 features. Most variables are numeric in nature, but the

    variables CurrentlyInGroup, IsBorrowerHomeowner, and IncomeVerifiable are bool variables. The dataset seem clean and easy to

    work with.The only wrangling i did was to adjust the datatypes to their appropriates like the datetimes and category before i

    went ahead with my exploration.

The relationship between¶

  • LoanOriginalAmount and EmploymentStatus

  • The relation between Income range and Term of loan

  • Loan Original Amount and Monthly Loan Payment

  • Employed people can get larger loan amounts compared to all other categories

  • It is noticed that EmploymentStatus and StatedMonthlyIncome affect features like BorrowerAPR and ProsperScore

  • In all income ranges people prefered to take a loan with 36 months probably because it gives them a high prosper score and/or

    good credit grade for future loan application.

  • The relationship between loan original amount and monthly loan payment is strongly positive in both heat map and scatter plot. Which shows that with monthly loan payment you are more likely to pay off loans in record time.

The intertwining and outcome between Income Range & Loan Original Amount & Term¶

  • This shows that in each income range the loan original amount increases the median of 60 months term more than 12 and 36 months term medians
